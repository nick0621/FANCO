    Four nuclear companies already walked this path

    Advanced nuclear and SMR companies at different stages, on different continents, chose the same platform — and each one switched on the layer its stage demanded, then expanded as milestones arrived. Identities withheld for confidentiality.

    Peer A · Adopted 2020

    U.S. advanced reactor developer

    Scaling engineering and regulatory programs
    • Core financial management with a multi-entity structure from day one
    • 35 full users across finance and operations
    • Purchasing controls, inventory and production operations
    • Dedicated test environment and premium support
    Signal: switched on operational depth early because hardware programs were already in motion.
    Peer B · Adopted 2021

    U.S. reactor developer, demo-plant stage

    Demonstration plant and commercialization path
    • Advanced financial controls with multi-entity structure
    • 32 full users plus self-service access for the wider team
    • Purchasing controls and project accounting
    • Test environment, premium support and ongoing advisory services
    Signal: project accounting became essential the moment engineering moved toward construction and demonstration.
    Peer C · Adopted 2022

    European nuclear scale-up

    International expansion and major fundraising
    • Core financial management and purchasing controls
    • Multi-entity structure with added country and currency coverage
    • 13 users plus implementation users
    • Dedicated test environment
    Signal: multi-country, multi-currency and procurement controls arrive fast when a scale-up raises across borders.
    Peer D · Now public

    U.S. advanced fission company

    Crossed from venture-backed to publicly listed
    • Financial control foundation in place before going public
    • Public-company close and reporting discipline
    • Scaled users and controls through the transition
    Signal: the IPO didn't require an ERP replacement — the platform FANCO would start on is the one peers took public.

    The pattern across all four: switch on the control foundation first, then add project, multi-entity, treasury and IPO reporting when business events create the need — never before.
